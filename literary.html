Literary Criticism: Psychoanalytic Theory

According to Sigmund Freud, Austrian psychologist, human behavior is driven by the parts of the mind known as the id, the ego and the superego.

Id: primitive and instinctual part of the mind that contains hidden memories and aggressive and sexual drives. The id demands that every impulse should be satisfied immediately, regardless of consequence.

Superego: While the Id contains our primal desires, the superego operates as a moral conscience, regulating our every actions and desires. While the Id demands short-term goals and ignores consequences, the Superego strive for the most favorable outcome in the long term.

Ego: Realistically, no human can ever operate either on the Superego or the Id primarily, they operate on the Ego, which is the middle ground for the desires of the Id and the Superego. The Ego ensure that action driven by impulses are expressed in acceptable manners and ensure moral action still satisfy personal needs.
